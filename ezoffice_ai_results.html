17 How do companies manage asset maintenance and depreciation? Companies manage asset maintenance and depreciation through a combination of operational practices and accounting methods. Here’s how they typically handle each: ### Asset Maintenance 1. **Preventive Maintenance**: Companies implement schedules for regular maintenance tasks to prevent breakdowns and prolong the life of assets. This can include routine inspections, servicing, and part replacements. 2. **Predictive Maintenance**: Using technology like IoT sensors and analytics, companies predict when an asset might fail and perform maintenance right before that happens. This approach minimizes downtime and repair costs. 3. **Computerized Maintenance Management Systems (CMMS)**: These systems help track maintenance activities, schedules, and costs. They improve efficiency by automating work orders and maintenance logs. 4. **Training and Procedures**: Companies invest in training employees to properly use and maintain equipment, which helps in reducing wear and tear. 5. **Outsourcing**: Some companies outsource maintenance to specialized firms to leverage expertise and reduce costs. ### Asset Depreciation 1. **Straight-Line Depreciation**: This method evenly spreads the cost of an asset over its useful life. It’s simple and widely used for assets that don’t lose value quickly. 2. **Declining Balance Method**: This accelerates depreciation, allowing for higher expenses in the early years of an asset’s life. It’s useful for assets that quickly lose value or become obsolete. 3. **Units of Production Method**: Depreciation is based on usage metrics, such as hours of operation or production output. This method aligns depreciation expense with actual use. 4. **Sum-of-the-Years’-Digits Method**: Another accelerated depreciation method that applies a changing rate over the asset’s life, resulting in higher initial depreciation. 5. **Component Depreciation**: Some companies depreciate components of a larger asset separately, particularly when different parts have varying useful lives. 6. **Regular Review and Adjustment**: Companies periodically review asset values and useful lives to ensure that depreciation reflects current conditions and economic realities. By managing maintenance efficiently and applying suitable depreciation methods, companies can optimize asset performance and financial reporting, ensuring that both operational and accounting objectives are met.

19 How can I keep a log of maintenance and usage for all company equipment? Keeping a detailed log of maintenance and usage for company equipment is crucial for effective asset management, prolonging equipment life, and ensuring operational efficiency. Here are some methods and tools you can use: 1. **Digital Solutions:** - **CMMS (Computerized Maintenance Management System):** Software like UpKeep, Fiix, or Maintenance Connection can automate maintenance scheduling, track usage, and keep detailed logs. These systems also provide dashboards and analytics to help with decision-making. - **Asset Management Software:** Tools like Asset Panda or EZOfficeInventory can track both maintenance and usage while offering features like barcode scanning and mobile app access. - **Spreadsheets:** If software solutions are not feasible, a well-organized spreadsheet can also serve as a maintenance log. Use columns for equipment ID, maintenance dates, performed tasks, parts used, service provider, and next scheduled maintenance. 2. **Manual Logs:** - **Logbooks:** Keep a physical logbook near equipment where staff can record usage and maintenance manually. This is more prone to human error and inefficiency compared to digital solutions. - **Forms:** Use printed forms that staff fill out and later enter into a digital system for tracking. 3. **Mobile Applications:** - Utilize mobile apps that staff can use to record data in real-time, facilitating immediate entry and minimizing errors associated with manual logging. 4. **RFID/Barcode Systems:** - Equip assets with RFID tags or barcodes and use scanners or apps to log usage and maintenance activities automatically. 5. **Regular Audits and Reviews:** - Schedule regular audits to ensure logs are accurate and up-to-date. Use this information to assess equipment performance and maintenance effectiveness. 6. **Training and Policies:** - Train employees on the importance of logging maintenance and usage. Clearly define procedures and responsibilities to ensure consistency and compliance. 7. **Integration with Other Systems:** - If possible, integrate maintenance logs with other enterprise systems like ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning) for a comprehensive overview of asset management. Choosing the right method depends on your company size, budget, and complexity of equipment management needs. Digital solutions are often favored for their efficiency, scalability, and ability to provide insights through data analytics.
20 What’s the best way to track assets in the field? Tracking assets in the field can be effectively managed using several modern technologies and methods. Here are some of the best options: 1. **GPS Tracking**: Using GPS tracking devices is a popular method for real-time location tracking of assets. These devices can be attached to vehicles, machinery, or other movable assets to provide continuous location data. 2. **RFID Tags**: Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) tags are useful for tracking assets within a certain range. RFID readers can scan these tags to maintain an inventory of assets and their locations. 3. **IoT Devices**: Internet of Things (IoT) devices can provide not only location data but also other information such as temperature, humidity, and usage statistics, which can be crucial for monitoring the condition of the assets. 4. **Barcodes/QR Codes**: For simpler or more budget-friendly solutions, barcodes or QR codes can be attached to assets. Scanning these with mobile devices can update their status and location in a centralized database. 5. **Asset Management Software**: Many companies offer comprehensive asset management software that integrates various tracking technologies and provides features such as inventory management, maintenance scheduling, and reporting. 6. **Mobile Apps**: Employees in the field can use mobile apps to manually update asset status and location. These apps often integrate with asset management systems to provide real-time data updates. 7. **Drones**: In larger fields or difficult-to-access areas, drones can be used to visually inspect and track assets. When choosing a method, consider factors such as the size and type of assets, budget, required data accuracy, environmental conditions, and integration with existing systems. Combining multiple technologies can often provide the most robust solution.
